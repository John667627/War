        <h2>World War I</h2>
        <p>World War I (1914-1918) was one of the deadliest conflicts in human history, involving many of the world's great powers. It reshaped national boundaries and led to significant changes in global politics.</p>

        <h2>World War II</h2>
        <p>World War II (1939-1945) remains the most widespread and deadliest conflict in history, leading to the downfall of Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan, and significantly altering the political landscape of the 20th century.</p>

        <h2>The Cold War</h2>
        <p>The Cold War (1947-1991) was a prolonged period of political tension between the Soviet Union and the United States, marked by the threat of nuclear war and numerous proxy conflicts around the globe.</p>

        <h2>Economic Consequences</h2>
        <p>War often leaves economies in shambles, leading to widespread poverty and infrastructure destruction, as well as long-term financial burdens from reconstruction efforts.</p>

        <h2>Humanitarian Consequences</h2>
        <p>Millions of lives are lost or displaced during war. Refugees are forced to flee their homes, while others suffer from lasting trauma due to the horrors they witnessed or endured.</p>

        <h2>Technological Impact</h2>
        <p>Although war brings devastation, it also drives technological innovation, especially in the fields of medicine, communication, and transportation.</p>
